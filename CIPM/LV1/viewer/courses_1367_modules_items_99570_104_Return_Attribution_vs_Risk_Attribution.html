1.04 | Return Attribution vs. Risk Attribution

Module 1: Return Attribution1.04 | Return Attribution vs. Risk Attribution

Learning Outcome

The candidate should be able to:

  • distinguish between return attribution and risk attribution

Complementary to return attribution is risk attribution. Whereas return attribution analyzes the consequences of active investment decisions on returns, risk attribution analyzes the risk consequences of such decisions. Depending on the purpose of the analysis, risk may be viewed in absolute or benchmark-relative terms. For example, when risk relative to a benchmark is the focus, a risk attribution analysis might identify and evaluate a portfolio’s deviations from a benchmark’s exposures to risk factors. Risk attribution is presented in detail in a separate learning module.

Performance attribution is defined to include return attribution and risk attribution (although in practice “performance attribution” is frequently used to just mean “return attribution”).

Attribution, including return attribution and risk attribution; often used as a synonym for return attribution.
