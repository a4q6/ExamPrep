Using Your Program Dashboard

The Program Dashboard gives you the ability to track your progress through the curriculum, assess your performance on practice questions, and build a study plan according to your schedule and learning priorities. Here’s how it works: 

Click on the Programs tab on the left global navigation bar to access the Dashboard. You will see three tabs: Progress and Performance, Study Planning and Settings.

Progress & Performance

In the Progress & Performance tab, you can quickly see how much of the curriculum you’ve already studied and your performance on the practice questions.  

 

Topics: On the left of the page, you’ll find Topics. The progress bar shows how many modules you’ve completed in the given topic. As you work through the modules and the lesson pages within them, your percent complete bar will grow. Click the links to go straight to the curriculum for each topic.

On the right side of each topic, there is an overview of the number of associated practice questions you’ve answered and a circle showing the percentage you’ve answered correctly out of the number of questions you’ve attempted. 

Module Progress and Practice Questions: Expand the Module Progress dropdown to view more details for each topic, including the number of modules, your progress through them, the number of questions answered out of total questions available, and the percentage you’ve answered correctly. On the far right is a Practice button that will take you straight to the practice questions for that module. You can also access the all practice questions by clicking on the Practice icon on the gray global navigation menu on the left at any time. 

 

 

Mock Exams: If you scroll down the list of Topics, you will find the Mock Exam area that provides you with information about the Mock Exams you’ve taken and a link to go to them on the Practice page. You can also reach the Mock Exams by navigating to the Practice area using the global navigation menu. (This section will be empty if there are no mock exams currently available.) 

Practice Pack: If you’ve purchased the Practice Pack, the additional  practice questions and Mock Exams will be included in the Practice area. These items will be included automatically in your question totals and progress statistics. 

 

Study Planning

The second tab on the Program Dashboard takes you to the study planning tool. It enables you to create a personalized calendar to study as much of the content as you wish, in the time you have. These features can enhance your learning experience with structure, flexibility, and time management. 

 

Progress Tracking: View upcoming due dates and track your progress through each course and module, ensuring your studies stay on schedule. 

Customization: Use the tool to prioritize and order your the study topics according to your preferences and needs.  

Time Management: Allocate your study time effectively, ensuring you have enough time to complete each module and review the material. 

Flexibility: Exclude the topics you feel confident about and rebuild your study plan as needed.  

PDF Generation: Generate a PDF of the study plan to have a clear overview of your start and end dates, weekly deadlines, and estimated completion times.

Try building a study plan yourself using the tool on the Study Planning tab to see how it works. You can rebuild your study plan as many times as you want. 

 

Dates: First, select your Start and End Dates in the tool. Consider ending the plan a few weeks before your exam date, if your time allows, for review and practice. Note that if you have less time available, the study plan will include more content into each day’s assignment. You will need to adjust your study focus accordingly. 

Unavailable Weeks: On this page, you can block out weeks for vacations, holidays, work conferences, review weeks, or other times you are not available to study. This section is optional. 

Organize Topics: You can set the order of topics to match your learning preferences. We present the content in the order in which it appears in the CFA Program curriculum, but you may choose to rearrange the content by dragging the topics using the six dots on the left of each one. You may also choose to exclude certain topics in which you feel confident. (Note that all topics will still appear on the exam regardless of whether they’ve been excluded from your study plan.) When you rebuild your study plan, you can choose whether to include topics you've already completed to study them further. This section is optional.  

Review and Build: Check this summary page to ensure everything looks correct and then click Build Study Plan. You can always rebuild it if needed.

Now that you’ve built your study plan, it is ready for you to use. Your screen will now show My Selected Study Topics and My Excluded Study Topics (if you’ve excluded any). 

My Selected Study Topics 

Topics: Under Topics, you will find the topics listed in the order you chose. You can click on a topic to go directly to the course material, or you can open the dropdown to see the modules inside. Beneath each module is a reading time estimate based on the word count and difficulty of the text. Because the time is an estimate, you may proceed through the content slower or faster than the provided number and adapt accordingly. 

Complete By: This column lists out the due dates for each assigned module. If a task is due within the current week, it will say Due Soon. If the date has passed and you have not reviewed all the pages, it will say Overdue. If you realize that you are falling behind or leaping ahead, you may want to rebuild your study planner to adjust. 

Progress: As you view the pages within the modules, the progress bar will grow. 

My Excluded Study Topics 

This section lists the topics that have been excluded from your study plan. You can still click on them to go straight to the content for review. As a reminder, excluded topics will still appear on the exam. You can add an excluded topic back to your study plan by rebuilding your plan. 

Download: At the top of the Study Planning tab, there is a Download button so you can save your study plan as a PDF or print it. It includes the study assignments, task deadlines, and completion time estimates. 

Rebuild: Once you’ve created a study plan, you can refresh by pressing the Rebuild button. Note that your previous study plan will not be saved. If you’d like to keep it, simply download it before rebuilding. 

Calendar: In the global navigation menu, there is a calendar icon. Your plan is displayed here, showing what’s due each day and flagging any overdue tasks. You can change the calendar view by clicking Week, Month, or Agenda in the top right. Click on any of the calendar items to see more details about that day’s assignment. You can go straight to the topic area by clicking into the assignment’s details. 

 

 

Settings

The third tab on the Program Dashboard allows you to control notifications. Use the toggles provided to enable these features to help you keep track of your learning. Note that both options are turned off by default. 

 

 

Study Plan Banner Notifications: If turned on, these will display a snapshot of what’s due this week for quick reference at the top of the Study Planning tab. 

 

 

Study Plan Email Notifications: This will enable weekly emails reminding you of your upcoming study tasks.
