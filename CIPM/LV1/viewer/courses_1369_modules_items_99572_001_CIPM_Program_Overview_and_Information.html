3. CIPM Program Overview and Learning Outcome Statements

CIPM Level I Program Overview

The CIPM Program builds your knowledge across several key areas of performance measurement. Level I emphasizes the conceptual foundations of performance measurement, attribution, appraisal, and presentation, as well as the GIPS® standards. The curriculum is organized into topics, each represented as a course in our Learning Ecosystem, and each topic contains learning modules. You can access each of these courses through your Learner Dashboard. 

Ethical and Professional Standards

Ethical and Professional Standards addresses ideas and concepts which underscore the importance of ethical behavior in the investment industry. Areas of focus include ethics and trust in the investment industry, the CFA Institute Code of Ethics and Standards of Professional Conduct and Standards of Practice Handbook, guidance for adherence to the Standards, Global Investment Performance Standards (GIPS®) and ethics application. 

Overview and Return Measurement

Overview and Return Measurement focuses on core skills in calculating and interpreting portfolio returns and risk. Topics include data quality, time-weighted and dollar-weighted return methods, benchmark selection basics, and an overview of GIPS® composite construction.

Return Attribution and Benchmark Analysis

Return Attribution and Benchmark Analysis explores the building blocks of risk measurement and risk attribution analysis. You’ll learn single-period equity and fixed-income attribution basics, plus how to reconcile portfolio returns against a chosen benchmark.

Risk Measurement, Risk Attribution and Security Characteristics

Risk Measurement, Risk Attribution, and Security Characteristics addresses contrasting perspectives on risk, the many ways to measure it. Equity portfolios are analyzed for style and attribution effects.

Performance Appraisal

Performance Appraisal covers essential appraisal metrics and ratios for evaluating risk-return characteristics. You’ll get an introduction to Sharpe, Treynor, Information ratios, and learn how to compare manager performance against peers and benchmarks.

Investment Performance Presentation

Investment Performance Presentation focuses on the process of constructing performance presentation reports and production of performance reports for internal and external audiences. Discover how to apply introductory GIPS® reporting guidelines and communicate results clearly to support client understanding.

Learning Outcome Statements

The CIPM Program Learning Outcome Statements (LOS) are the backbone of the program. They outline the specific knowledge and skills that you will need to demonstrate in each topic area. You can use the LOS to help guide your study efforts by focusing on the subjects you would like to strengthen.

4. Tools and Technologies

Computer Hardware and Peripherals

The information provided below relates to most standard desktop and laptop computers. Many devices, including tablets and mobile phones, offer a high level of access to most of the functions in the courses. Access to all functions of the courses may be best experienced by using a computer (desktop or notebook) that meets the requirements described below.

  • A computer that supports the most recent versions of web browsers. It is recommended to use a computer five years old or newer with at least 1GB of RAM.
  • Speakers or ability to use headphones for audio playback.
  • (Optional) A printer is recommended as needed to download and print material. Not required.

Canvas

For best performance, Canvas can be accessed using Windows, Mac, Linux, iOS, Android, or any other device on the current or previous major releases of Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari.

5. Accessibility

Our Commitment to Accessibility Standards

At CFA Institute, we are deeply committed to fostering an inclusive and accessible educational experience for all. We recognize the significance of providing equitable access to our curriculum materials and proactively seek ways to enhance and improve our accessibility features.

The World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) develops standards and guidelines to help everyone build a web based on the principles of accessibility, internationalization, privacy, and security. We strive to ensure that our curriculum materials comply with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2 AA published by the W3C. We recognize the importance of accessible digital content, and we are fully committed to adhering to all applicable laws pertaining to accessibility.
